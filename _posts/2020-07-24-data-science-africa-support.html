Summer School on Machine Learning and Data Science

The summer school on machine learning and data science targeted graduate students, researchers and professionals working with large quantities of data. The sessions were spread across 4 days, 24th, 25th, 28th and 29th of July 2020.

The following 19 topics were covered;

Introduction to Python & Jupyter Environment Classification and Regression
Visualizing high dimensional data with PCA and tSNE Data Visualization : Communicating Insights and Results Effectively
Anomaly Detection MD4SG: Mechanism Design for Social Good
Introduction to Process Mining: Data Science in Action Global Challenges in Economics and Computation
Introduction to Spatial Data Analysis Deep Learning Practicals
Data for Good Computer Vision
Natural Language Processing Social Media Data Analysis
Trans-African HydroMeteorological Observatory (TAHMO) Machine Learning Systems, Streaming Data
Exploring R for Spatial Analytic Ethics in ML for Health
Data Engineering in Integrity/Security

Workshop (30th July and 1st August)

The workshops were organized around presentations of how application of data science methods are applied to problems relevant to Africa and last year in particular saw more presentations around agricultural solutions among other topics in the areas including: healthcare, social media, geospatial modelling, economic monitoring, resume writing, Internet Of Things and data science for the Sustainable Development Goals.

A panel discussion on Data Science Africa Uganda Network was conducted. The panel discussion consisted of a dialogue on the benefits of growing local data science chapters that would duplicate the activities of data science to students, researchers and professionals at a local level (by region and districts) in Uganda.

Achievements of DSA since 2015

Some of the achievements that DSA has attained with the Labs collaboration since 2015 include:

  • Organised week long events in six African countries including Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Ghana, Nigeria and Ethiopia. End of this year these countries will have increased to eight with South Africa and Senegal.
  • DSA has given out research grants each worth 10,000USD to 10 research teams across Africa, after a competitive application process with more 121 applications
  • Attracted high level data science expertise from academia, private sector, civil society, governments, UN and NGO to instruct in summer schools and showcase their work in workshops - at their own cost travel and accommodation.
